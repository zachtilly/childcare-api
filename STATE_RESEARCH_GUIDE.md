# State-by-State Research Guide

This guide provides specific URLs and instructions for finding policy data for priority states.

## How to Use This Guide

1. Open the URLs for your target state
2. Look for the specific sections mentioned
3. Fill values into the CSV template (see BULK_IMPORT_TEMPLATE.csv)
4. Import using the bulk import script

## CALIFORNIA (38.9M population)

### Primary Resources
- **Main Agency:** California Department of Social Services (CDSS)
- **Website:** https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/child-care
- **CCDF Plan:** https://www.acf.hhs.gov/occ/form/ccdf-plans (search for California)
- **Licensing:** https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing

### Metric-by-Metric Instructions

**Income Eligibility Threshold (% of FPL)**
- Source: CA CCDF Plan, Section 3.1 "Eligibility Criteria"
- Look for: "Families at or below [X]% of State Median Income" or Federal Poverty Level
- URL: Search CCDF plan for "income eligibility"

**Subsidy Rates**
- Source: CA CDSS Regional Market Rate Survey
- URL: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/child-care-funding
- Look for: "Regional Market Rate" schedule, find infant care rates

**Staff Ratios**
- Source: Title 22, California Code of Regulations
- URL: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/ord/entres/getinfo/pdf/cclman.pdf
- Look for: Chapter 1, Section 101216.1 (infant ratios)

**Paid Family Leave**
- Source: CA Employment Development Department
- URL: https://edd.ca.gov/en/disability/paid-family-leave
- Look for: "How long can I receive PFL benefits?"

**Worker Wages**
- Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics
- URL: https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_ca.htm
- Search for: SOC Code 39-9011 (Childcare Workers)

**Tax Credits**
- Source: Franchise Tax Board
- URL: https://www.ftb.ca.gov/forms/2023/2023-3506-instructions.html
- Look for: Child and Dependent Care Expenses Credit

### Primary Resources
- **Main Agency:** Texas Health and Human Services (HHS)
- **Website:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/services/financial/child-care-services

- **Licensing:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/doing-business-hhs/provider-information/protective-services-providers/child-care-licensing

### Metric-by-Metric Instructions

**Income Eligibility**
- Source: TX CCDF Plan or HHS website
- URL: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/services/financial/child-care-services/child-care-services-handbook
- Look for: Section on income eligibility, typically 85% of State Median Income

**Subsidy Rates**
- Source: TX HHS Rate Information
- URL: Search "Texas child care rates" on HHS site
- Look for: Payment rates by age group and county

**Staff Ratios**
- Source: Texas Minimum Standards for Child Care
- URL: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/laws-regulations/forms/adoption-foster-care-child-care-minimum-standards
- Look for: Chapter 746 (Centers) or 747 (Homes), infant ratios

**Paid Family Leave**
- Texas does not have state-mandated paid family leave
- Value: 0 weeks

**Worker Wages**
- Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics
- URL: https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_tx.htm
- Search for: SOC 39-9011

**Tax Credits**
- Texas has no state income tax, therefore no state child care tax credit
- Value: $0

## Quick Reference: Where to Find Each Metric

### Income Eligibility (% of FPL)
1. Check state CCDF plan (Section 3 usually)
2. Look for "85% State Median Income" or "X% Federal Poverty Level"
3. State child care subsidy website eligibility page

### Subsidy Rates (Monthly, Infant)
1. State child care rates/reimbursement page
2. Market rate surveys
3. Provider payment schedules
4. Convert weekly to monthly if needed (multiply by 4.33)

### Staff-to-Child Ratios
1. State licensing regulations
2. Administrative code (search "[State] child care licensing regulations")
3. Look for infant or 0-12 month category
4. Format as "1:4" (1 staff per 4 infants)

### Annual Inspections
1. Licensing regulations
2. Usually required - set to "true" unless specifically stated otherwise
3. Look for "frequency of inspections" or "monitoring"

### Centers Per 1000 Children
1. Child Care Aware state fact sheets
2. State early childhood data systems
3. Difficult to find - use estimates if needed

### Child Care Deserts (%)
1. Center for American Progress reports
2. Search "[State] child care desert"
3. CAP has interactive maps

### Paid Family Leave (Weeks)
1. State labor department
2. Search "[State] paid family leave"
3. Only 13 states have programs (as of 2024)
4. Others: 0 weeks

### Child Care Tax Credit
1. State department of revenue/taxation
2. Search "[State] child care tax credit" or "dependent care credit"
3. States without income tax (TX, FL, etc.): $0

### Worker Wages
1. Bureau of Labor Statistics
2. https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_[state].htm
3. SOC Code: 39-9011 (Childcare Workers)
4. Use mean annual wage

### Training Hours
1. State licensing regulations
2. Professional development requirements
3. Look for "continuing education" or "annual training"

## Research Tips

1. **CCDF Plans are goldmines** - Most comprehensive single source
2. **Use site search** - Most state sites have terrible navigation, use search
3. **Check effective dates** - Policies change, note when data is from
4. **Save PDFs** - Download source documents for reference
5. **Note discrepancies** - If sources conflict, note it and use most recent
6. **Contact agencies** - When stuck, call the state agency directly
